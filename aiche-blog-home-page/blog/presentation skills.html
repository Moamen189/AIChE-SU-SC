Presentation Skills

Hate public speaking? You're not alone. In fact, most people dislike public speaking to a degree from simply feeling a bit nervous, to full on glossophobia (fear of public speaking).

Reasons for fear of public speaking:

1.Lack of self-confident:

This is probably the most frequently named reason for performance anxiety. Two strategies will help:

-Remember that the people in a sizable audience are exactly the same ones you talk to individually.
-Know the content you're explaining well.

2.Lack of experience:

You have to stick to any opportunity to present (if you are good at something, find some people who are interested in that and explain it then value your performance).

Three steps to be a good presenter:

  • Prepare.
  • Practice.
  • Present.

    • Prepare:

    1-Know your audience:

    Research your audience before you start preparing the speech or presentation.
    You should know their Education level, Age ranges and Gender.
    You can find information about them from their managers, peers, surveys, general demographic sources, their personal web pages and more. Sometimes you can even talk with them and ask them questions such as what kinds of presentation they like.

    2-Define the basic idea:

    You must define the basic idea of your presentation so that your audience can summarize the topic that you will explain and come out with the most benefit.
    In addition to the basic idea, you need to specify the sub-points that you will explain.
    Then you select your indirect messages, Example;(If you will present to your manager, your goal will be to inform him that you are interested in the topic and you did many researches, so you will put photos, statistics and research from reliable sources).

    3-Organize your time:

    Organize the time of every point in order not to Exceed the specified time of the presentation.

    4-Credibility:

    Use evidence that they find credible.
    Facts and figures, respected authorities, charts and graphs.

    Practice:

    By rehearsing in front of a group of people, giving your speech and seeing their reactions is a great way to boost your confidence in your material and delivery.

    Whether it's a small group of your friends, co-workers, family or even a random group of strangers, the action of giving your presentation allows you to see reactions and get natural human feedback.

    This will calm your nerves and make you more comfortable with the entire experience. Many times, you'll find that your worst mistakes will surface on this first go-around, leaving you with plenty of time and feedback to correct mistakes and re-organize your thoughts.

    Or by Video yourself by hearing and seeing yourself, you can judge the inflection, speed and enunciation of your voice.


    Things to look for:

    Be on your nature.
    Self-confident.
    Extensive knowledge about the whole subject.



    Present:

    Calm yourself from the inside:

    Nervousness causes physiological reactions which are mostly attributed to the increase of adrenaline in your system. You can counteract these effects with a few simple techniques:
    Practice deep breathing.

    Drink water.

    Smile.

    Speak more slowly than you would in a conversation and leave longer pauses between sentences.

    Move around during your presentation. This will expend some of your nervous energy.

    Stop thinking about yourself. Remember that the audience is there to get some information and it is your job to put it across to them.

    Attractive Start:

    Start with a story as People may not remember what you said in your speech but they will remember your stories.
    Start with provocative questions or by shock.


    Body Language:

    Facial expression

    Smile when you say something funny and vice versa.

    Gestures

    Use hand gestures to emphasis your words.
    Keep the audience's attention by varying your gestures, incorporating your head, arms and hands.

    Posture

    Keep a good posture, stand straight with shoulders back, relaxed and feet shoulder width apart. Do not cross your arms, put your hands in your pocket or slouch.

    Eye contact

    Moving from face to face, making eye contact while speaking ensures that the audience is engaged. When answering an audience member's question maintain eye contact, this conveys sincerity and credibility.

    Movement

    Move around the presentation space, your speech will be more dynamic.
    Use movement to illustrate transitions from one subject or key point to another.
    Stepping towards the audience creates a positive feeling, use this technique when you want to encourage or persuade your audience.

    Voice

    Your voice shouldn't be too little or too loud.

    Optical instruments:


  • Keep it Simple.
  • Limit bullet points & text.
  • Limit transitions & builds (animation).
  • Use high-quality graphics.
  • Choose your fonts well.
  • Use video or audio.
  • Don't read from it.
  • Use rules.

    • Be on time:


    Arrive early and get your equipment set up and start on the agreed date.

    Unexpected situations:

    Someone asks you a question during the presentation, you can deal it with:


    Tell them that you will open the discussion after ending or tell them from the beginning that questions are at the end.


    Someone tells you that you are wrong, you can deal it with:


    Discuss the matter with him until you reach a satisfied conclusion.


    Some people talk to each other, you can deal it with:


    Ask any attractive question in order to regain their attention.
