Polyethylene

From plastic bottles to plastic food containers, polyethylene is commonly used as the material of choice for many plastic items. But what is polyethylene? What is it made up of? How to process PE? For which requirements is it a material of choice?


What is Polyethylene?

Polyethylene is a lightweight, high-quality plastic that has a variable crystalline structure. It's commonly used in various applications such as films, tubes, plastic components, and laminates. It is one of the most widely produced plastics in the world (tens of millions of tons are produced worldwide each year).

How is Polyethylene Made?

Polyethylene is made by addition or radical polymerization of ethylene (olefin) monomers. (Chemical formula of Ethene - C2H4). Ziegler-Natta and Metallocene catalysts are used to carry out polymerization of polyethylene.

Common Types of Polyethylene (PE)

PE is classified by its density and branching. The most common types of polyethylene are:

1- Branched Versions



Low-density polyethylene (LDPE)
Linear low-density polyethylene (LLDPE)

2- Linear Versions

High-density polyethylene (HDPE)
Ultra-high-molecular-weight polyethylene (UHMWPE)

3- Cross-linked polyethylene (PEX or XLPE)



In addition, PE is also available in other types such as:

1- Medium-density polyethylene (MDPE)

2- Very-low-density polyethylene (VLDPE)

3- High-molecular-weight polyethylene (HMWPE)

4- Ultra-low-molecular-weight polyethylene (ULMWPE)

5- Chlorinated polyethylene (CPE)


Applications of High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE)

1- Packaging applications including crates, trays, bottles for milk and fruit juices, caps for food packaging, jerry cans, drums, industrial bulk containers etc.
2- Consumer goods like garbage containers, housewares, ice boxes, toys.
3- Fibers and textiles like ropes, fishing nets, and sport nets.


Applications of Low-Density Polyethylene (LDPE)

1- Packaging, it is used in packaging industry for pharmaceutical and squeeze bottles, caps and closures, tamper evident, liners, trash bags, films for food packaging (frozen, dry goods, etc.), laminations etc.
2- Pipes and Fittings, it is used to manufacture water pipes and hoses for the pipes and fittings industry.



How to Process PE?

High density polyethylene can be easily processed by injection molding, extrusion, blow molding and roto molding.
The most common processing technique used for Low Density Polyethylene is extrusion. Low Density Polyethylene can be processed by injection molding or roto molding also.
