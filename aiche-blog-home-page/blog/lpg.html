Liquefied petroleum gas (LPG)

What is LPG?

LPG is a product of refining. Mainly, it consists of propane (C 3 H 8 ) and butane (C 4 H 10 ), but may contain some unsaturated components like propene (C 3 H 6 ) and butene (C 4 H 8 ). So the composition of LPG is not fixed. At typical ambient temperatures, LPG is liquid at about 8 bars. At the higher end of typical ambient temperatures, the pressure remains moderate (the vapor pressure of propane at 55Â°C is about 20 bar), which enables economic storage. LPG is denser than air. Consequently, in the case of leaks, LPG generates a layer upon the ground and does not easily let lighter gases escaping (natural gas, hydrogen, etc.). This characteristic promotes the formation of ignitable mixtures near to the ground in the case of leaks in the fuel system, and consequently, for explosion protection, special attention is required to avoid leakages.


Composition of LPG

The percentage of propane and butane depends on the weather, as in colder regions we use more propane, unlike in hotter places(it is related to their molecular weight). This helps to keep the storage pressure more independent from regions or different seasons or-as we call- ambient conditions.

Uses of LPG

1-Rural heating.
2-Motor fuel.
3-Refrigeration.
4-cooking.
5-conversion to gasoline.
As LPG is used as a main fuel, it is also used as an ignition fuel to support next liquid fuels operation, or as an alternative for fuel oil. Storage of fuel oil is simpler and more attractive than LPG, as secondary fuel, however. In comparison tonatural gas, LPG has a higher adiabatic flame temperature, which has an impact on NO emissions. Moreover, it has a lower autoignition temperature,which is of relevance concerning flashback in premix combustion systems.

Pros and cons of LPG


1- It's a gas under normal pressure and temperature.
2- It's easy to be compressed.
3- It has no odor, so we add a little percentage of Ethanethiol.
4- It can't be heated, otherwise, it can explode because it's flammable.
5- Easy to be transported.
